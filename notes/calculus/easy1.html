Calculus Made Easy

1. To Deliver You From The Preliminary Terrors

  • d - "a little bit of"
    • these little bits are considered to be indefinitely small
    • dx means a litte bit of x
    • dt means a litte bit of t
  • \(\int\) - "the sum of all the little bits of", "the whole of"
    • \(\int dt\) means the sum of all the little bits of t
    • if t is considered to be made up of a lot of little bits, each of which is called dt, the sum of all these little bits gives us the whole of t
    • if t=1 hour, you can think of it as cut up into 3600 little bits called seconds. (i.e. dt=1 second ). The whole of the 3600 little bits added up together make up one hour. (i.e. \(\int dt = dt+dt+dt+...+dt \ (3600 \ dt's \ added) = 1 \ hour\))
