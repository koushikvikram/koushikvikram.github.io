1. Getting Started Taming Text

Data Science  =  Product Management + Software Engineering

Product interview questions test your ability to think critically on improving and innovating products.

Product Interview Question Guide

Why are there product data science type interview questions?

The general Product Roadmap for any product in tech has a team for every single product/feature.
The Product Manager is in charge of the product/feature and sets up the roadmap, deciding what to build, test and launch.
The Data Scientist is there to help support all decisions that have to be made and measure a lot of the performance.

What does the Data Scientist actually do with the Product Manager?

  1. Look into the data to find insights
  2. Pull data on Dashboard
  3. Build feature with Engineers
  4. A/B test (and launch)
  5. Roll out and measure success
  6. Build another dashboard

A lot of these tasks involve collaborating on product. Data Scientists are meant to have a lot of business intuition to be able to influence the product. Most of the times, the product manager doesn't know what to ask or what to look into. They have multiple other responsibilities and a lot of people surrounding them. The Data Scientist is the "guardian of the data".

They are supposed to know
  • What to look for
  • What to investigate
  • How to measure the success of any product

What are the Product Data Science interview questions?

There are generally 5 different categories of questions.
  1. Investigating Metrics - "What happens when feature X drops by Y percent?"
  2. Measuring Success - "How would you measure the success of this feature/product?"
  3. Feature Change - "Let's say we want to add/change/improve a new feature to product X, what metrics would you track to make sure it's a good idea?"
  4. Metrics Tradoff - "You're the Product Manager for Facebook Reactions. Comments are down by 10% but reactions are up by 15%. These two metrics are going in opposite directions. How do you deal with it?" Network effects.
  5. Growth - "Let's say we want to grow X metric on Y feature. How would we go about doing so?"
Think big, take a step back and ask for clarification before you dive into these questions.

The most important thing you can do to prepare

USE THE PRODUCT. Remember that your interviewer is likely testing you on knowledge that you do not have as a non-employee of the company. So, in order to catch up, you need to do as much product research as you can.

Thinking about the product as a person that works at the company is very different from just using the product on a regular basis. Treat the interview like a business consultation.

For example, for a company like LinkedIn, you can think of questions like:
  1. How does LinkedIn make money?
  2. What are some features that LinkedIn has released? Why do you think they released them?
  3. Which features on LinkedIn drew you into the website? Which features drew you into a particular product?
  4. Why would a person pay money to use a certain service on LinkedIn?

Skills companies look for when tackling product data science interview questions

  1. Structured Problem Solving
  2. Decision Making in Ambiguity
  3. Prioritization Framework
  4. Impact Sizing
  5. Stakeholder Management
  6. Strategic Thinking
  7. General Business Intuition

Framework for product questions

  1. Clarify the question - product goals? background context?
  2. Make assumptions - to narrow the scope
  3. Analyze User Flows - how do users get to a certain feature? how do they use it? what types of users are there?
  4. Define hypothesis - helps understand the root cause of an issue
  5. Draw metrics to support your hypothesis - helps you prioritize
  6. Tie your analysis back to your product goals
  7. Think out loud.
